# Day 3 findings — proving the Sydney revenue drop

Generated by running `sql/03_sydney_drop_analysis.sql` against the loaded dataset.
July 2026 is excluded everywhere — it's a partial month (data generation stops
at "today"), so it always looks anomalous regardless of any real trend.

**Regenerated after the Day 5 data-generation patch.** Orders can now only be
assigned to a customer after their `signup_date`, and the Sydney anomaly is
driven by two independent, tunable causes (acquisition decline + retention
decline) instead of a single blanket order-volume multiplier. The numbers
below are from the corrected dataset, not the original Day 3 run.

## 1. The drop is real, sharp, and starts exactly when the model says it should

| Month    | Revenue     |
|----------|------------:|
| 2026-02  | $184,443    |
| 2026-03  | $205,329    |
| 2026-04  | $180,946    |
| 2026-05  | **$93,946** |
| 2026-06  | **$112,447**|

Sydney sits in a normal $180k-205k band through April, then falls off a
cliff in May (-48% vs April) and stays down in June (-38% vs April). This
lines up exactly with `ANOMALY_MONTHS = 2` and an anomaly window starting
2026-05-01 in `notebooks/nb_config.py` — the SQL independently rediscovers
a fact we know is true by construction, which is the sanity check you want
before trusting a pipeline on real data.

## 2. Every other city stayed flat over the same window

| City      | Apr 2026  | May 2026  | Jun 2026  |
|-----------|----------:|----------:|----------:|
| Adelaide  | $214,243  | $198,957  | $223,682  |
| Brisbane  | $212,211  | $211,457  | $240,679  |
| Canberra  | $204,295  | $226,398  | $205,910  |
| Melbourne | $204,546  | $223,394  | $211,838  |
| Perth     | $220,912  | $194,966  | $204,324  |
| **Sydney**| **$180,946** | **$93,946** | **$112,447** |

Every non-Sydney city stays within its normal +/-10% band across the same
three months. This rules out a global seasonal effect (e.g. a slow month
for the whole business) — whatever happened, it's Sydney-specific.

## Conclusion

The drop is real, it's isolated to Sydney, and it starts precisely at the
anomaly window boundary. Day 4 decomposes *who* drove it — acquisition,
retention, order frequency, or spend per order.
